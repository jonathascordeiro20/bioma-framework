# B.I.O.M.A. — Editions (Fair-Source Core)

**🌐 English · [Português](EDITIONS.pt-BR.md)**

B.I.O.M.A. follows a **fair-source** model: the core is source-available under the
**Functional Source License (FSL-1.1-MIT)** — free to read, run, audit and build on for any
non-competing purpose, and it converts to MIT two years after each release. A separate
**Enterprise** edition, under a commercial license, adds the sovereignty, compliance and
support layer that regulated organizations need.

## Community Edition — this repository, FSL-1.1-MIT

Source-available. Read it, audit it, run it, embed it in your own product — at no cost, for any
**Permitted Purpose** under the [`LICENSE`](LICENSE). The one thing you may **not** do is a
**Competing Use** — repackaging B.I.O.M.A. itself as a rival product or service. Two years
after each release, that version automatically becomes MIT.

- `bioma_micro` — the Rust micro-kernel: hormonal bus, context apoptosis, `saturation_scan`.
- `bioma` — `CognitiveFirewall` (secret redaction, cognitive-DDoS mitigation, timeout guard),
  provider-agnostic client, local server.
- Full test suite, benchmarks, and documentation.

**Everything the ground-truth benchmarks prove (−80–97% tokens, secret redaction,
cognitive-DDoS mitigation, μs kernel) is in the free Community edition.**

## Why fair-source

Source-available drives **adoption, trust and credibility** — anyone can read and audit the
code — while the FSL's non-compete clause stops a rival from taking it and reselling it against
you. The Enterprise edition **funds development**. Using the free core never obligates you to
Enterprise — Enterprise exists for organizations that need the sovereignty/compliance/support
layer on top.
